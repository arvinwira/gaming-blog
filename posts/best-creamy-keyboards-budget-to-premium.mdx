### Features and Performance
The AULA F75 Pro is currently one of the undisputed champions of the extreme budget category. This 75 percent layout keyboard packs an absurd amount of value into a very affordable package.

Out of the box, it sounds absolutely phenomenal. It comes equipped with pre lubed LEOBOG Reaper switches that deliver a sublime, creamy mechanical sound. The keyboard features a highly advanced structure utilizing an extended integrated silicone pad and PCB single key slotting. This better optimizes resilience and stability, making the hand feel softer and more elastic. Five layers of filling silencer completely counteract the cavity noise of the switch hitting the positioning plate.

The build quality is shocking for the price. It features tri mode wireless connectivity via Bluetooth 5.0, a 2.4GHz wireless dongle, and USB wired connection. You can connect up to five devices at the same time. The 4000mAh battery provides excellent longevity. It also includes a premium feeling metal volume knob and stunning side printed PBT keycaps. These gradient black side engraved caps take a double injection molding and heat sublimation process, making them sweat proof and incredibly durable while allowing the 16.8 million color RGB to shine through beautifully.

### Features and Performance
For budget conscious gamers who demand elite competitive performance, the Gamakay NS68 is an absolute anomaly. This is a wireless Hall Effect magnetic keyboard that absolutely destroys the competition in its price bracket.

Hall Effect switches use magnets to detect keystrokes instead of physical metal contacts. The NS68 utilizes Outemu Peach Crystal Magnetic Switches, allowing you to customize the exact actuation point of every single key from 0.1mm to 3.7mm in 0.01mm steps. It features a blistering wired 8KHz polling rate and a wireless 1KHz polling rate, offering an ultra low latency of 0.125ms. Features like Snap Tap, Mod Tap, Toggle Key, and Dynamic Keystrokes are all baked in.

While budget Hall Effect boards usually sound terrible and hollow, the NS68 actually feels quite solid. The acoustic profile leans a bit clackier out of the box due to the magnetic switches, but because it is fully hot swappable, you can easily modify it. The ABS OEM profile shine through keycaps look great with the 16.8 million RGB backlighting.

### Pros & Cons
> **Pros**: Unbeatable competitive performance for the price, high-precision magnetic switches, ultra-low latency, hot-swappable PCB.
>
> **Cons**: Sound profile is clackier than "creamy" out of the box, plastic feel, RGB visibility can be weak in bright light.

## The Ultimate Modding Guide for Creamy Acoustics
Even if you buy a premium prebuilt keyboard, there is always room for massive improvement. The mechanical keyboard hobby is all about personalization and chasing the perfect sound. If you want to push your acoustic profile to the absolute limit of buttery smoothness, you need to apply some basic modifications.

Do not be intimidated by opening your keyboard. Most modern boards are designed to be modified. Here are the most effective mods to make any keyboard sound creamier.

### The Tempest Tape Mod
This is the easiest, cheapest, and most effective acoustic mod ever invented. You simply take apart your keyboard and apply one to three layers of standard paper masking tape directly to the back of the printed circuit board.

The tape acts as an acoustic low pass filter. It reflects the deeper sound waves back up toward the keycaps while completely absorbing high pitched, scratchy frequencies. This instantly makes the keyboard sound richer, poppier, and significantly creamier. Always use a low adhesion paper tape like blue painters tape. Never use duct tape or electrical tape.

### Lubricating Switches and Stabilizers
While many switches come factory lubed in 2026, manual hand lubing is still the absolute gold standard for acoustic perfection. You will need a switch opener tool, a small precision brush, and a vial of Krytox 205g0 lubricant.

Carefully opening the switch and applying a very thin, consistent layer of lube to the plastic stem rails and the internal metal spring completely eradicates any microscopic plastic friction. This turns a decent linear switch into a perfectly silent, floating glide. For stabilizers, applying thick dielectric grease to the ends of the metal wire will cushion the impacts and eliminate the dreaded ticking noise.

### Plate Materials and PE Foam
The material that holds your switches in place has a massive impact on sound. Stiff materials like brass or aluminum create a harsh, high pitched clack. If your keyboard supports alternate plates, swap it out for a Polycarbonate or an FR4 fiberglass plate. These softer plastics absorb vibration and drastically soften the sound profile.

Adding a sheet of ultra thin PE foam directly between the switches and the circuit board is the final secret ingredient for the creamy sound. This foam creates a highly concentrated popping sound that enthusiasts refer to as the "marbly effect."

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the exact difference between a thocky keyboard and a creamy keyboard?**
While both terms describe pleasant, non clicky sounds, they sit on different ends of the frequency spectrum. A thocky keyboard produces a very deep, bass heavy, and resonant thud. A creamy keyboard produces a softer, muted, and more balanced sound, like smooth, rounded raindrops hitting plastic.

**Do I absolutely need linear switches to get a creamy sound?**
Linear switches are highly recommended. They travel straight down without physical bumps, providing the smoothest acoustic curve. Tactile switches create a sharp bump that leads to a clackier sound profile, though heavily modified tactiles can sound decently muted.

**Is it safe to perform the tape mod on a wireless keyboard with a battery?**
Generally yes, but use caution. Standard paper masking tape is non-conductive, but if your battery gets warm during fast charging, thick tape can trap heat. Use standard paper masking tape and avoid covering battery ventilation areas or microchips.

**Why do mainstream gaming keyboards usually sound worse than custom keyboards?**
Mainstream brands often prioritize gaming features like ultra-fast polling or RGB over acoustics. They may use thinner plastic cases and tray mounts to reduce costs. Custom makers prioritize thicker aluminum, gasket mounting, and premium dampening foams which produce superior sound.

**Are Hall Effect magnetic switches good for typing, or just for gaming?**
Modern 2026 models like the Keychron Q1 HE use dual-rail stems and heavily dampened internals, making them exceptionally smooth and quiet. They are now fantastic for both competitive gaming and long-form typing sessions.
